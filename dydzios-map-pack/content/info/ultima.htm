Ultima - A Heroes 3 Map
Version 1.0   4/30/01





I'll be honest - I like overkill.  Huge, obscenely powerful armies obliterating each other, zillions of creatures, high level heroes, powerful artifacts, all that good stuff.  And I like Ultima.  The whole series, ever since playing the very first Ultima on an ancient Apple ][.  Even Pagan.  Yes, there, I've said it.  I liked Pagan.  So there.  But anyway, I thought it'd be cool to bring Britannia to Heroes, so here it is.
 

Requirements:
Heroes of Might and Magic III
Heroes of Might and Magic III: Armageddon's Blade
Heroes of Might and Magic III: Shadow of Death
-or-
Heroes of Might and Magic III Complete

Download the map here (150k)
 

Installation Instructions:
Unzip the map file into the "maps" directory in your Heroes III directory.  Run Heroes III.  Pick the "Ultima" map.  Play.  Enjoy.
 

Map details:
- XL Map, 2 levels
- 8 Players, all available to play as human
- 8 unique viewpoints on the events of the world
- A Horde of events that should amuse hardcore Ultima fans
- Lots of custom heroes from the various Ultimas
- All the combo artifacts (I know it sounds munchkin-like, but you really won't get more than one or two each time you play unless you go far out of your way)
- Bonus quests that are sometimes difficult, but won't take your heroes away from the action for too long
 

For the Parents:
Although it contains some twisted humor and makes evil sound like fun, it's basically pretty clean.  We'll rate it about a PG-13.
 

The Players:
- Each player has a different personality and perspective on the events that occur during the game.
- Most players are (plotwise) generally evil, since otherwise they wouldn't fight each other.
- Some players have an easier time than others, just due to their location on the map.  Don't expect balance.
- Everyone has a primary town, with a secondary town that's not too hard to find, always the same town type as the primary.

Here are the different sides:

Red - Terfin, home of Great Evil
Blue - The Castle of Our Liege Lord British
Tan - The Ruins of Prideful Magincia, now home only to corpses
Green - Empath Abbey, bastion of love
Orange - The Lycaeum, home of the greatest scholars in the land
Purple - The Great Stygian Abyss
Teal - The Militant Keep of Serpent's Hold
Pink - Secret, Underground Ambrosia

Comments on Locations (minor spoilers)
 

The Dungeons:
Every major Ultima dungeon is in the game, and I've tried to make each dungeon special in some way (usually consistent with at least one Ultima) so they don't get too boring, so there's almost always a theme.  Also, each of the dungeons contains a combination artifact broken into pieces and guarded by strong monsters.  Every starting town is near a dungeon, and some island starting locations were so cramped that they have some of their mines underground.

Other Stuff:
Custom Heroes start with slightly improved stats (except Katrina - she's "special").  Use them.  Also, we actually bothered to give all the custom heroes a short bio, so check those out (click their picture in the Hero Window).  Here's a free sample featuring an enemy from Ultima 7:
"Poor misunderstood Horance.  All he wanted to do was extend his lifespan so he could continue his research into Sprituality, but something went tragically wrong and the towne of Skara Brae got a massive flaming enema.  Now he lives underground, neither living nor dead, and the greatest tragedy of all is that he has no servants."

There are a lots of tavern rumors, but for some reason HOMM3 picks its own lame rumors more often than I'd like.  Still, they're worth checking, as mine sometimes have valuable clues.

There are a few B5 references here.  No, make that a few shameless B5 references here.  I figure they made the entirety of Serpent's Hold into a ST:TNG reference in U7, so I get to have a sci-fi indulgence too.  Fans should recognize them instantly, but I think they stand well on their own.
Miscellaneous Spoilers
 

Disclaimers for the Ultima junkie:

Disclaimer One:
I took great liberties with the Ultima saga.  Many things are different, or in the wrong place, or completely made up, just because I thought it'd play better.  Any complaints (How come my favorite NPC isn't there?) will be read out loud using the voice of the comic-book guy on the Simpsons.

Disclaimer Two:
The premise of the plot is cheesy.  This isn't really a spoiler, since it happens to everyone on day one:  Some sort of time disturbance has happened so that I could justify putting elements from most of the Ultima series on the same map.  The rest of the plot is less cheesy, or at least somewhat amusing.
